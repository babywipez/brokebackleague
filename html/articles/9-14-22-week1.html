WEEK 1 RECAP

Week 1 is in the books. How did everyone fair?

by Chris Cassada - 9/14/22



Week 1 Standings


Regy 1 - 0

Avi 1 - 0

Chris 1 - 0

Eric 1 - 0

Al 1 - 0

Jake 1 - 0

Luke 0 - 1

Matt 0 - 1

Katie 0 - 1

Dillon 0 - 1

Dennis 0 - 1

Josh 0 -1


What a spectacular first week of fantasy football! There’s a ton to get into, so let’s not delay!



Al Squeezes His First Win Out


Well, my two titans of the league, Al and Katie, both earned a A+ from me in my team rankings. But, one of them had to take the L this week. This one was a nail biter all the way to the end. Kamara and Moore took big stinky poops in their pants for Katie while Al had to endure Pierce and Pollard barely being involved in their offenses. Luckily for Al, his 0 RB-ish strategy worked perfectly as he got carried to a W thanks to Allen and Jefferson almost exclusively. Jefferson looks like an absolute force to be reckoned with this season; everyone should be very afraid…


Dennis Almost Proves Me Wrong. Almost.


I was watching the Broncos Seahawks game, and my heart sank when I saw Jeudy’s huge touchdown catch. Was I totally wrong about Sutton? It remains to be seen what the split in Denver is going to be with their receivers, but Dennis looks pretty good so far taking Jeudy in the 3rd. Proud of him. Ja’Marr Chase is going to be Dennis’ best friend this season as he seems to be Burrow’s get-out-of-jail-free card. We can only imagine what Chase might put up when Burrow plays a good game! We both had a goose egg, mine from Irv Smith Jr. and him from Devonta Smith (what the hell happened there??) I got Pittman right, so I’ll take this mild victory lap in the meantime.


Josh Might Quit Fantasy Soon


I’m going to have to take Josh out for dinner or something because the way his fantasy luck seems to go is just plain fucked at this point. You couldn’t have painted a much worse picture for Josh’s team as his highest scorer was Ekeler - who everyone was mostly disappointed with after this week. Jake’s team had disappointing players too, however, as Aaron Rodgers has clearly taken one too many ayahuasca retreats this offseason. Ceedee’s outlook also looks scary with Dak missing significant time in the next several weeks. I think higher scoring weeks are in both of these team’s futures.


C? Watch This


Eric said in the group chat that my grade for his team was “generous,” but I’m not sure it was generous enough. Herbert, Kupp, and CEH joined together in establishing dominance week 1. CEH was probably the most surprising, and we can’t assume he’ll get touchdowns on a weekly basis, but he looks very improved from last season - and he’s finally getting some decent passing work. Dillon’s team had pretty underwhelming production across the board outside of the Steelers D/ST who dominated Burrow, forcing enough turnovers to open a bakery. Najee is questionable going into this week, and Russ did not look good at all. However, I see a bounceback game from both Aaron Jones and Kyle Pitts coming very soon.


The Upside King


I made it a point to talk about Regy’s upside in his fantasy team, but I got all the players wrong! Turns out, Saquon is, in fact, back. Kelce did his thing too, and the Dolphins D/ST ruined every plan Bellichick had going into this game. I’m going to chalk up Trey Lance’s game to the absolutely awful weather at Soldier Field. He still rushed the ball a ton, and I expect him to make a slight improvement in passing in better weather. Matt is laughing at me right now for criticizing his Mahomes pick as he absolutely dominated the position and reminded everyone why he’s the face of the NFL. Matt started JK Dobbins, who was inactive for this game, but it wouldn’t have mattered. Saquon made sure of that.


Stop The Count!


This was another really close game that came down to the wire in the Monday night game. I’d love to pat myself on the back with a Luke win, justifying my grade I gave Avi, but alas. (It must have been infuriating watching Rashaad Penny just refuse to get into the end zone time after time.) Avi had really solid production across the board, and he should be sitting pretty most weeks with his stable of volume. If you had told me before this week that Swift, Davante, and A.J. Brown would all get at least 20 points in fantasy, I would have bet the house on Luke winning. I’m still trusting Mike Williams moving forward, especially with Allen out for the time being. Avi will have a difficult decision to make with his RB / FLEX spots this week between Zeke, Sanders, Anderson, and (maybe?) Lazard. Seriously, what the hell was that Robbie Anderson bullshit about? WE WANT MORE MOORE BAKER!



I wanted to get this out yesterday along with a waiver wire breakdown, but I’ve just been kinda busy with school and drinking. Good luck to everyone in week 2!
